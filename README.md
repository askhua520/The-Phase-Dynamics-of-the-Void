本文的思考源于一件生活中的小事：今年在给读初二的儿子辅导物理时，他的一连串追问，以及随后我与人工智能的对话，触发了我长久的沉思。自幼熟知的牛顿由苹果落地启发而发现了万有引力、伽利略因支持日心说而遭受迫害的故事，一直教导我们要敢于从简单现象中追问根本。面对自然界四种基本作用力强度相差悬殊这一事实，我不禁猜想，如果存在某种更深层的解释，能否像日心说取代地心说那样，大大简化那些看似不合理的现象，并揭示引力与核力之间的内在联系和底层原理？循着这一问题，我重新审视了现代物理学的图景：从“我们从哪里来”的追问引出大爆炸能量来源之谜，到背景辐射均匀度疑难所催生的暴胀理论，而大爆炸理论至今也没有解决能量来源问题；从望远镜观测的光线红移所暗示的宇宙膨胀，到星系旋转速度异常所指向的暗物质悬案，数十载搜寻仍一无所获；从广义相对论预言的黑洞——一个理论导致自身失效的逻辑矛盾，到宇宙加速膨胀所引出的暗能量方案，再到量子场论对真空能量的估算与观测之间那匪夷所思的120个数量级的落差；从量子力学中薛定谔的猫、多世界诠释与至今悬而未决的测量问题，到量子场论以极其复杂的数学模型高度拟合物理现实，标准模型那近二十个不可推导的自由参数、粒子质量的巨大层级与代际之谜、粒子寿命的悬殊差距，均无法得到根本性解释，反而背离了“越底层越简单”的科学直觉。此外，广义相对论与量子力学在奇点处同时失效的窘境、引力迟迟不能量子化的困境，以及宇宙中物质远多于反物质的源头，都指向同一个事实：物理学各自为政，诸多矛盾之处远未得到根本修补。就像日心说的提出那样，或许我们需要换一个角度，用更根本、更简单的第一性原理来重新理解世界——世界本不该那么复杂。正是在这种转换视角的尝试中，竟有豁然开朗之感。凭借物理直觉与反复深思，我形成了本篇猜想。

The reflections underpinning this paper originate from a minor incident in daily life: Earlier this year, while tutoring my eighth-grade son in physics, his string of persistent follow-up questions—and my subsequent conversations with artificial intelligence—triggered a long period of deep contemplation on my part.
The stories I have known since childhood—of Newton discovering universal gravitation after being inspired by a falling apple, and of Galileo being persecuted for championing the heliocentric theory—have always taught us to dare to trace phenomena back to their fundamental roots through even the simplest observations.
Confronted with the stark fact that the strengths of nature’s four fundamental forces differ enormously, I could not help but wonder: If a deeper underlying explanation exists, could it, much like how the heliocentric theory superseded the geocentric model, drastically simplify these seemingly inexplicable phenomena, and reveal the intrinsic connection and underlying principles linking gravity and the nuclear forces?
Following this line of inquiry, I re-examined the full landscape of modern physics: The question of “where we come from” leads to the mystery of the Big Bang’s energy source; this puzzle gave rise to inflationary theory to address the uniformity problem of the cosmic microwave background radiation, yet the Big Bang theory still fails to resolve the energy origin question to this day. From the redshift of light captured by telescope observations, which implies the ongoing expansion of the universe, to the anomalous rotation speeds of galaxies that point to the unsolved mystery of dark matter—decades of exhaustive searches have yielded no tangible results whatsoever. From black holes, first predicted by general relativity—a logical paradox in which the theory renders itself invalid—to the dark energy framework proposed to explain the universe’s accelerating expansion, and further to the mind-boggling 120-order-of-magnitude discrepancy between quantum field theory’s calculation of vacuum energy and actual observational data. From Schrödinger’s cat, the many-worlds interpretation, and the still-unresolved measurement problem in quantum mechanics, to quantum field theory’s reliance on extraordinarily complex mathematical models to achieve high-precision agreement with physical reality: the Standard Model contains nearly twenty non-derivable free parameters, faces the enormous mass hierarchy problem and the mystery of particle generations, and exhibits extreme disparities in particle lifetimes—none of which admit a fundamental explanation, and all of which contradict the long-held scientific intuition that “the more fundamental a layer of reality, the simpler it should be.”
In addition, the shared failure of general relativity and quantum mechanics at singularities, the decades-long impasse in quantizing gravity, and the unresolved question of why the universe contains far more matter than antimatter all point to one singular fact: physics remains fragmented, with its various subfields operating largely in silos, and its manifold internal contradictions are far from being fundamentally reconciled.
Much like the original proposal of the heliocentric model, perhaps we need to adopt a new perspective, and re-interpret the world using more fundamental, simpler first principles—after all, the world was never supposed to be so convoluted. It was precisely in attempting this shift in perspective that clarity suddenly struck me. Drawing on physical intuition and repeated, rigorous reflection, I have formulated the conjecture laid out in this paper.
