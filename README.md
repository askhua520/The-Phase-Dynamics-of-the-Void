# The-Phase-Dynamics-of-the-Void
本文的思考源于一件生活中的小事：今年在给读初二的儿子辅导物理时，他的一连串追问，以及随后我与人工智能的对话，触发了我长久的沉思。自幼熟知的牛顿由苹果落地启发而发现了万有引力、伽利略因支持日心说而遭受迫害的故事，一直教导我们要敢于从简单现象中追问根本。面对自然界四种基本作用力强度相差悬殊这一事实，我不禁猜想，如果存在某种更深层的解释，能否像日心说取代地心说那样，大大简化那些看似不合理的现象，并揭示引力与核力之间的内在联系和底层原理？循着这一问题，我重新审视了现代物理学的图景：从“我们从哪里来”的追问引出大爆炸能量来源之谜，到背景辐射均匀度疑难所催生的暴胀理论，而大爆炸理论至今也没有解决能量来源问题；从望远镜观测的光线红移所暗示的宇宙膨胀，到星系旋转速度异常所指向的暗物质悬案，数十载搜寻仍一无所获；从广义相对论预言的黑洞——一个理论导致自身失效的逻辑矛盾，到宇宙加速膨胀所引出的暗能量方案，再到量子场论对真空能量的估算与观测之间那匪夷所思的120个数量级的落差；从量子力学中薛定谔的猫、多世界诠释与至今悬而未决的测量问题，到量子场论以极其复杂的数学模型高度拟合物理现实，标准模型那近二十个不可推导的自由参数、粒子质量的巨大层级与代际之谜、粒子寿命的悬殊差距，均无法得到根本性解释，反而背离了“越底层越简单”的科学直觉。此外，广义相对论与量子力学在奇点处同时失效的窘境、引力迟迟不能量子化的困境，以及宇宙中物质远多于反物质的源头，都指向同一个事实：物理学各自为政，诸多矛盾之处远未得到根本修补。就像日心说的提出那样，或许我们需要换一个角度，用更根本、更简单的第一性原理来重新理解世界——世界本不该那么复杂。正是在这种转换视角的尝试中，竟有豁然开朗之感。凭借物理直觉与反复深思，我形成了本篇猜想。

The reflections in this essay originate from a small incident in daily life: while tutoring my son in physics when he was in his second year of junior high school this year, his series of persistent questions, followed by my subsequent dialogue with an artificial intelligence, triggered a prolonged period of contemplation. The stories I have known since childhood—of Newton being inspired by a falling apple to discover universal gravitation, and of Galileo suffering persecution for supporting the heliocentric theory—have always taught us to dare to trace simple phenomena back to their fundamental roots. Confronted with the fact that the strengths of nature’s four fundamental forces differ so enormously, I cannot help but wonder: if a deeper explanation exists, could it, much like the heliocentric model supplanting the geocentric one, greatly simplify those seemingly unreasonable phenomena and reveal an intrinsic connection and underlying principle between gravity and the nuclear forces?
Pursuing this question, I re-examined the landscape of modern physics: from the question “Where do we come from?” leading to the enigma of the Big Bang’s energy source, to the inflation theory prompted by the puzzle of the uniformity of the cosmic microwave background—yet the Big Bang theory has still not resolved the issue of the energy source; from the redshift of light observed through telescopes implying an expanding universe, to the anomaly of galactic rotation speeds pointing to the unsolved mystery of dark matter, for which decades of searching have yielded nothing; from the black holes predicted by general relativity—a logical contradiction where a theory leads to its own breakdown—to the dark energy proposal brought forth by the discovery of the accelerating expansion of the universe, and on to the mind-boggling 120 orders of magnitude discrepancy between the quantum field theory estimate of vacuum energy and what is observed; from Schrödinger’s cat, the many-worlds interpretation, and the still unresolved measurement problem in quantum mechanics, to the highly complex mathematical models of quantum field theory that fit physical reality with great precision, the nearly twenty non-derivable free parameters of the Standard Model, the enormous hierarchy and generation puzzle of particle masses, and the vast disparity in particle lifetimes—none of these can be fundamentally explained, and all instead deviate from the scientific intuition that “the more fundamental, the simpler.”
Furthermore, the predicament where both general relativity and quantum mechanics fail simultaneously at singularities, the persistent difficulty of quantizing gravity, and the origin of matter vastly outnumbering antimatter in the universe all point to the same fact: physics operates in disconnected domains, and its many contradictions are far from being fundamentally mended. Just as the heliocentric theory was proposed, perhaps we need to shift our perspective and use more fundamental, simpler first principles to re-understand the world—the world should not be this complicated. It was precisely in this attempt to change perspectives that a sudden sense of clarity emerged. Relying on physical intuition and repeated deep contemplation, I formed the conjecture presented in this essay.
